World Economic Forum (WEF)

The institutional focal point for global governance and "Public-Private Cooperation."

Overview

The World Economic Forum (WEF) is an international non-governmental organization based in Cologny, Switzerland. It was founded on January 24, 1971, by German engineer and economist Klaus Schwab. The forum is best known for its annual meeting at the end of January in Davos, a mountain resort in the Swiss Alps.

WEF's stated mission is "improving the state of the world," which it seeks to achieve through the integration of corporate, political, and academic leaders into a unified decision-making platform.

Documented Facts

The WEF's membership includes the world's 1,000 leading companies. It also manages the "Young Global Leaders" (YGL) program, which has included prominent world leaders such as Emmanuel Macron, Justin Trudeau, and Angela Merkel.

Global Redesign Initiative: In 2010, the WEF launched the "Global Redesign Initiative," which proposed moving toward a model where global governance is managed by "stakeholder" coalitions rather than solely by national governments.
