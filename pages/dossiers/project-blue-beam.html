Project Blue Beam

Analyzing the theory of a planned global holographic deception.

Overview

Project Blue Beam is a theory first proposed by Quebecoise investigative journalist Serge Monast in the mid-1990s. It describes a sophisticated, four-step plan allegedly involving NASA and the United Nations to implement a New World Order by creating a fake, global religious event using advanced technology.

The core of the theory relies on the existence of secret holographic and mind-control technologies that could be used to simulate a celestial event visible across the entire world.

The Four Steps

  • Step 1: The breakdown of all archaeological knowledge via engineered earthquakes and "new" discoveries that discredit current religions.
  • Step 2: A massive "space show" involving three-dimensional optical holograms and sounds, projected into the atmosphere as a simulated second coming or alien arrival.
  • Step 3: "Telepathic Electronic Two-Way Communication" using ELF (Extremely Low Frequency), VLF (Very Low Frequency), and LF (Low Frequency) waves to bypass consciousness and speak directly to the mind.
  • Step 4: The "Universal Supernatural Manifestation via Electronic Means" to make humanity believe an alien invasion is imminent, forcing a global surrender to central authority.

Narrative Analysis

While often dismissed as pure science fiction, researchers in the "Great Awakening" community point to rapid developments in holographic projection (e.g., 7D holograms), voice-to-skull (V2K) technology, and the increasing push for a unified global response to potential "extraterrestrial" threats as indicators of the plan's feasibility.

"Project Blue Beam is the ultimate psychological operation—the use of man's own technological achievements to enslave his spirit through a manufactured divinity." - Serge Monast (Summary)

We track patents related to laser-induced plasma filaments (LIPF) and other technologies capable of creating three-dimensional images in the open air without a screen, which align with the technical requirements of the "Space Show" phase.
