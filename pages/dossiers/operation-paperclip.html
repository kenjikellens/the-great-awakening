Executive Summary

Operation Paperclip was a secret intelligence program led by the Joint Intelligence Objectives Agency (JIOA) in which more than 1,600 German scientists, engineers, and technicians were recruited from former Nazi Germany for government employment in the United States following World War II. The program’s primary objective was to secure German military and scientific expertise before it could be captured by the Soviet Union. To achieve this, the JIOA systematically "whitewashed" the Nazi party and SS records of these recruits.

The personnel recruited under Paperclip became the primary architects of the U.S. aerospace program (NASA), the ballistic missile defense system (ICBMs), and modern biomedical/chemical research frameworks.

The Mechanics of Whitewashing: JIOA & The Paperclip

The program’s name, "Paperclip," refers to the paperclips attached to the personnel files of scientists whose Nazi records had been scrubbed of incriminating data. The Office of Strategic Services (OSS)—the precursor to the CIA—and the JIOA actively altered documentation to ensure that key scientists who were "security risks" could obtain the necessary clearances for American military research.
