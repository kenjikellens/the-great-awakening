Executive Summary

Operation Paperclip was a secret intelligence program led by the Joint Intelligence Objectives Agency (JIOA) in which more than 1,600 German scientists, engineers, and technicians were recruited from former Nazi Germany for government employment in the United States following World War II. The program’s primary objective was to secure German military and scientific expertise before it could be captured by the Soviet Union. To achieve this, the JIOA systematically "whitewashed" the Nazi party and SS records of these recruits to bypass President Truman’s explicit order that no active Nazi supporters be allowed into the country.

Research conducted by the Great Awakening project identifies Operation Paperclip as the **Foundation of the Modern Technocratic State**. It established the precedent of "Scientific Exceptionalism"—the idea that high-level technological progress justifies the suspension of moral and legal accountability. The personnel recruited under Paperclip became the primary architects of the U.S. aerospace program (NASA), the ballistic missile defense system (ICBMs), and modern biomedical/chemical research frameworks.

The Mechanics of Whitewashing: JIOA & The Paperclip

The program’s name, "Paperclip," refers to the paperclips attached to the personnel files of scientists whose Nazi records had been scrubbed of incriminating data. The Office of Strategic Services (OSS)—the precursor to the CIA—and the JIOA actively altered documentation to ensure that key scientists who were "security risks" could obtain the necessary clearances for American military research.

Documented File Alterations:

  • SS Membership Suppression: Removing evidence of participation in the Allgemeine-SS and other elite paramilitary structures.
  • Slave Labor Denial: Systematically erasing references to the use of slave labor from concentration camps (Mittelbau-Dora) in the production of Nazi weaponry.
  • Employment Continuity: Fabricating "consultancy" records to make it appear that the scientists were apolitical researchers rather than active participants in the Third Reich’s war machine.

Wernher von Braun: SS-Sturmbannführer & NASA Legacy

Wernher von Braun is the most visible figure of Operation Paperclip. As the primary developer of the V-2 rocket (the world’s first long-range guided ballistic missile), Von Braun was essential to U.S. interests. Declassified records confirm that Von Braun held the rank of SS-Sturmbannführer (Major) and was a recurring guest of Heinrich Himmler.

Despite his proximity to the Mittelwerk underground factory—where an estimated 20,000 slave laborers died from exhaustion and abuse while building V-2s—Von Braun was transferred to the U.S. in 1945. He subsequently became the first director of NASA’s Marshall Space Flight Center and the chief architect of the Saturn V rocket that took humans to the moon. This transition demonstrates the Complete Integration of Nazi aerospace ideology into the core of the American scientific establishment.

Hubertus Strughold: The Father of Space Medicine

Hubertus Strughold was the director of Nazi Germany's Institute for Aviation Medicine. Brought to the U.S. under Paperclip, he became the chief scientist for the U.S. Air Force’s Aerospace Medical Division and is hailed as the "Father of Space Medicine."

In 2025-2026, intensified scrutiny of Strughold’s war-time activities led to the removal of his name from several prestigious awards. Investigations by the Justice Department (IWG) identified his direct oversight of unethical human experiments at Dachau, including high-altitude and hypothermia tests. His legacy serves as the primary case study for the Biological Continuity between Third Reich pseudo-science and modern "life extension" and "bio-security" research.

The Gehlen Organization: Foundation of Modern Intelligence

While Paperclip focused on scientists, its intelligence counterpart recruited Reinhard Gehlen, the former head of Nazi military intelligence for the Eastern Front. The "Gehlen Organization" was funded entirely by the U.S. Army and the CIA until 1956, eventually becoming the BND (German Federal Intelligence Service).

The Gehlen Org recruited hundreds of former Gestapo and SS officers, under the justification that their "expertise" on the Soviet Union was indispensable.
