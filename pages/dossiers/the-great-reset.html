The Great Reset

A global initiative to reshape economic and social systems post-2020.

Overview

The **Great Reset** is a proposal by the World Economic Forum (WEF) to rebuild the economy sustainably following the COVID-19 pandemic. It was unveiled in June 2020 by Klaus Schwab, the founder and executive chairman of the WEF. The initiative seeks to facilitate a "new social contract" that honors the dignity of every human being.

The Three Pillars

Klaus Schwab outlined three main components of the Great Reset aimed at creating a more resilient global system:

1. Stakeholder Capitalism: Steering markets toward fairer outcomes by improving coordination in tax, regulatory, and fiscal policies.

2. ESG-Driven Investment: Ensuring that recovery funds and private investments advance shared goals, such as equality and sustainability.

3. The Fourth Industrial Revolution: Harnessing technological innovations to address social and health challenges, impacting every aspect of human life through digitalization.

Stakeholder Capitalism & ESG

The core philosophy of the Great Reset is Stakeholder Capitalism, which posits that corporations should serve the interests of all stakeholders—not just shareholders. The mechanism for enforcing this shift is the ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) scoring system, which creates a non-financial metric for corporate compliance and global standard alignment.

Research Note: Critical Analysis

While presented as a humanitarian effort, independent researchers track the "Great Reset" as a vehicle for the centralization of global economic power. Key areas of concern include the influence of the "Young Global Leaders" program on national governments and the potential for programmable digital currencies to restrict individual spending based on ESG compliance.
