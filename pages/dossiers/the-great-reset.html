Executive Summary

The Great Reset is a high-level strategic initiative launched by the World Economic Forum (WEF) in June 2020, aimed at rebuilding the global economic and social systems in the aftermath of the COVID-19 pandemic. According to its primary architect, Klaus Schwab, the reset is not merely a recovery plan but a fundamental restructuring of the "social contract" that governs modern society. The initiative seeks to transition the world from shareholder capitalism to "stakeholder capitalism," leveraging the technological breakthroughs of the Fourth Industrial Revolution (4IR) to achieve global sustainability and social equity.

Research conducted by this platform analyzes the Great Reset as the definitive administrative toolkit for the implementation of Agenda 2030. It provides the private-sector mechanisms (ESG, Digital ID, and CBDCs) necessary to enforce the UN’s Sustainable Development Goals. As of 2026, the Great Reset has moved from its conceptual phase into an operationalized "Global Redesign" period.

The 2020 Manifesto: Resetting the Global Contract

The launch of the Great Reset was characterized by a rare convergence of high-level endorsements, including then-Prince Charles (now King Charles III), the Managing Director of the IMF (Kristalina Georgieva), and the Secretary-General of the UN (António Guterres). In his foundational text, "COVID-19: The Great Reset", Schwab argues that the pandemic provided a "unique window of opportunity" to rethink and reboot the world order before its collapse.

The "Reset" logic posits that the failures of 2020 (supply chain fragility, health system collapse, and inequality) were symptoms of an outdated "un-managed" globalization. The solution proposed is **Managed Globalization**, where a "cooperative" network of governments, corporations, and civil society organizations (the multi-stakeholder model) manages human behavior and resource distribution through algorithmic, standardized frameworks.

Stakeholder Capitalism & The Davos Manifesto

A central tenant of the Reset is Stakeholder Capitalism. Unlike traditional Capitalism, which prioritizes the interests of company owners (shareholders), Stakeholder Capitalism requires corporations to serve "all stakeholders"—including employees, customers, suppliers, and "the environment." To measure progress toward this goal, the WEF, in collaboration with the "Big Four" accounting firms, developed the International Business Council (IBC) Metrics.

While marketed as an ethical upgrade, researchers note that Stakeholder Capitalism effectively transforms corporations into quasi-governmental regulators. By using ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) scores as a requirement for market access, the WEF and its partners can enforce global policy (such as carbon rationing or diversity quotas) without the need for national legislation or democratic consensus.

The Fourth Industrial Revolution: Bio-Digital Convergence

The technological engine of the Great Reset is the Fourth Industrial Revolution (4IR). This era is defined not by individual digital tools, but by the "blurring of the boundaries between the physical, digital, and biological worlds." The WEF’s 2025-2026 whitepapers on Bioconvergence outline a future where human biology is directly integrated into the digital economic network.

  • The Internet of Bodies (IoB): A network of smart devices (wearable, injectable, or ingestible) that collect real-time biometric data, enabling "personalized" medicine and insurance through the Global Digital Compact.
  • Neural Interfaces: Research into brain-computer interfaces (BCIs) aimed at "enhancing" human cognition and enabling total workplace monitoring.
  • Precision Bio-Editing: CRISPR and other genetic technologies utilized for the "biological redesign" of the workforce and the environment.

This convergence is the biological counterpart to the Digital ID requirement. In a fully reset economy, a citizen's "biological status" (vaccination records, DNA health markers, and real-time stress levels) becomes a factor in their ESG score and their ability to participate in the "stakeholder" economy.

The Young Global Leaders (YGL) Network Analysis

The implementation of the Great Reset is facilitated through the WEF’s Young Global Leaders (YGL) and Global Shapers programs. Established in 2004, the YGL program identifies and trains potential leaders in politics, business, and media to adopt the Forum’s "Stakeholder" worldview.

Klaus Schwab famously stated in 2017 at the Harvard Kennedy School: "We penetrate the cabinets. I know that half of [the Canadian] cabinet are for our Young Global Leaders." Research indicates that YGL graduates consistently champion WEF-aligned policies in their respective nations, including Digital ID mandates, CBDC trials, and 15-minute city zoning. This creates a global synchronization of policy that bypasses traditional national political divisions.

Circular Economy: The "You Will Own Nothing" Narrative

A widely documented aspect of the Great Reset is the transition to a Circular Economy or "Product-as-a-Service" model. This was epitomized by the 2016 WEF video/article featuring Ida Auken titled "Welcome to 2030: I Own Nothing, Have No Privacy and Life Has Never Been Better."

Under this model, private property is systematically replaced by subscription-based services. In the "Reset" economy, individual ownership of cars, homes, and even basic tools is viewed as "inefficient" and "unsustainable." The resulting dependency on corporate providers for daily necessities ensures high levels of compliance—if a citizen’s ESG score falls below the required threshold, their "as-a-service" access (to transportation, energy, or housing) can be algorithmically revoked.
