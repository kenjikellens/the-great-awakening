The Great Reset

Analyzing the World Economic Forum's strategic initiative to restructure the global economic and social contract through technological integration and "stakeholder capitalism."

Executive Summary

The Great Reset is a high-level strategic initiative launched by the World Economic Forum (WEF) in June 2020, aimed at rebuilding the global economic and social systems in the aftermath of the COVID-19 pandemic. According to its primary architect, Klaus Schwab, the reset is not merely a recovery plan but a fundamental restructuring of the "social contract" that governs modern society. The initiative seeks to transition the world from shareholder capitalism to "stakeholder capitalism," leveraging the technological breakthroughs of the Fourth Industrial Revolution (4IR).

Research conducted by this platform analyzes the Great Reset as the definitive administrative toolkit for the implementation of Agenda 2030. It provides the private-sector mechanisms (ESG, Digital ID, and CBDCs) necessary to enforce the UN’s Sustainable Development Goals. As of 2026, the Great Reset has moved from its conceptual phase into an operationalized "Global Redesign" period.

The 2020 Manifesto: Resetting the Global Contract

The launch of the Great Reset was characterized by a rare convergence of high-level endorsements, including then-Prince Charles (now King Charles III), the Managing Director of the IMF, and the Secretary-General of the UN. In his foundational text, "COVID-19: The Great Reset", Schwab argues that the pandemic provided a "unique window of opportunity" to rethink and reboot the world order before its collapse.

The "Reset" logic posits that the failures of 2020 were symptoms of an outdated "un-managed" globalization. The solution proposed is **Managed Globalization**, where a "cooperative" network of governments, corporations, and civil society organizations manages human behavior and resource distribution through algorithmic, standardized frameworks.

Stakeholder Capitalism & The Davos Manifesto

A central tenant of the Reset is Stakeholder Capitalism. Unlike traditional Capitalism, which prioritizes shareholders, Stakeholder Capitalism requires corporations to serve "all stakeholders"—including employees, suppliers, and "the environment."

While marketed as an ethical upgrade, researchers note that Stakeholder Capitalism effectively transforms corporations into quasi-governmental regulators. By using ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) scores as a requirement for market access, the WEF and its partners can enforce global policy without the need for national legislation or democratic consensus.

The Fourth Industrial Revolution: Bio-Digital Convergence

The technological engine of the Great Reset is the Fourth Industrial Revolution (4IR). This era is defined by the "blurring of the boundaries between the physical, digital, and biological worlds." The WEF’s 2025-2026 whitepapers on Bioconvergence outline a future where human biology is directly integrated into the digital economic network.

  • The Internet of Bodies (IoB): A network of smart devices (wearable, injectable, or ingestible) that collect real-time biometric data.
  • Neural Interfaces: Research into brain-computer interfaces aimed at "enhancing" human cognition and enabling workplace monitoring.
  • Precision Bio-Editing: CRISPR and other genetic technologies utilized for the "biological redesign" of the workforce.

This convergence is the biological counterpart to the Digital ID requirement. In a fully reset economy, a citizen's "biological status" becomes a factor in their ability to participate in the "stakeholder" economy.

The Young Global Leaders (YGL) Network Analysis

The implementation of the Great Reset is facilitated through the WEF’s Young Global Leaders (YGL) and Global Shapers programs. Established in 2004, the YGL program identifies and trains potential leaders to adopt the Forum’s "Stakeholder" worldview.

Klaus Schwab famously stated in 2017: "We penetrate the cabinets." Research indicates that YGL graduates consistently champion WEF-aligned policies in their respective nations, including Digital ID mandates, CBDC trials, and 15-minute city zoning. This creates a global synchronization of policy that bypasses traditional national political divisions.

Circular Economy: The "You Will Own Nothing" Narrative

A widely documented aspect of the Great Reset is the transition to a Circular Economy or "Product-as-a-Service" model. This was epitomized by the 2016 WEF narrative: "You will own nothing, and you will be happy."

Under this model, private property is systematically replaced by subscription-based services. In the "Reset" economy, individual ownership is viewed as "inefficient." The resulting dependency on corporate providers ensures high levels of compliance—if an ESG score falls, access to essential services can be algorithmically revoked.
