Executive Summary

BlackRock and Vanguard are the two largest asset management firms in the world, collectively managing over $20 trillion in assets as of 2026. Through their massive exchange-traded fund (ETF) offerings and institutional mandates, these firms hold significant voting blocks in nearly every publicly traded company in the S&P 500. This "Universal Ownership" model grants them unprecedented influence over global corporate policy, ranging from executive compensation to environmental and social targets (ESG).
