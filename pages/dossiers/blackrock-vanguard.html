BlackRock & Vanguard

The "Invisible Monopoly" controlling the majority of global trade, media, and technology through institutional ownership.

Overview

BlackRock and Vanguard are the two largest asset management firms in the world. As of 2024, they collectively manage approximately $20 trillion in assets. Due to their role as "passive" investors in index funds, they have become the majority shareholders in almost every major corporation — from pharmaceutical giants and tech conglomerates to major media outlets and energy producers.

The Aladdin System

BlackRock's proprietary risk management platform, Aladdin (Asset, Liability, Debt and Derivative Investment Network), monitors and manages an estimated $21 trillion in assets globally. This system provides BlackRock with a level of data-driven market insight that is unprecedented in financial history, allowing them to anticipate and shape economic trends before they manifest in retail markets.

ESG & Behavior Modification

Through BlackRock's aggressive promotion of ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) metrics, asset managers have begun using their voting power to force corporations into adopting specific social and political policies. CEO Larry Fink’s annual letters to CEOs have explicitly stated that "behaviors must change," signaling a transition from "Shareholder Capitalism" (profit-driven) to "Stakeholder Capitalism" (policy-driven).

Critical analysis views this as a "Corporate Social Credit System," where companies that do not comply with the prevailing technocratic narrative are denied access to institutional capital.

Research Note

We track the overlapping board memberships between BlackRock, Vanguard, and the State Street Corporation (the "Big Three"). This research focuses on the Circular Ownership phenomenon where these firms are often the top shareholders in each other and in the major rating agencies that determine global credit scores.
