USS Liberty Incident

The 1967 attack on a US Navy research ship during the Six-Day War.

The Event

On June 8, 1967, during the Six-Day War, the USS Liberty, a technical research ship (spy ship) operating in international waters off the coast of the Sinai Peninsula, was attacked by Israeli Air Force jet fighter aircraft and Israeli Navy motor torpedo boats.

The attack involved napalm, machine gun fire, and torpedoes. Despite being clearly marked as a US vessel, the ship was crippled and suffered heavy casualties.

Documented Facts

The attack lasted for approximately 75 minutes. The Israeli government later apologized for the attack, stating it was an error due to mistaking the ship for an Egyptian vessel (the El Quseir).

Survivor Testimony: Survivors consistently maintained that the ship was clearly flying the American flag and that the weather was clear, making it impossible for the attackers to have mistaken the ship's identity.

Narrative Analysis

Researchers analyze the USS Liberty incident as a potential "False Flag" that failed. The narrative suggests that the attack was intended to completely sink the ship and leave no survivors, after which the event could be blamed on Egypt to draw the United States into the conflict on the side of Israel.

"The attack was not an accident. The ship was targeted deliberately, and the order to stand down came only after it became clear the ship would not sink quickly." - Independent Analysis

Research Note: Suppression of Evidence

The US government's official inquiry (the 1967 Court of Inquiry) has been criticized for being "a cover-up from start to finish" by some involved, including the ship's survivors and various intelligence officials.
