Analyzing the 1967 attack on the U.S. Navy technical research ship by Israeli forces in international waters, and the subsequent suppression of evidence.

Executive Summary

On June 8, 1967, during the Six-Day War, the USS Liberty, a technical research ship belonging to the U.S. Navy, was subjected to a sustained, 75-minute attack by Israeli fighter jets and motor torpedo boats. The vessel was operating in international waters off the coast of the Sinai Peninsula. The attack resulted in the deaths of 34 American crewmen and the wounding of 171.

The attack was a coordinated effort to silence the U.S. Navy’s most advanced SIGINT (Signals Intelligence) platform in the Mediterranean.

Israel claimed they mistook the USS Liberty for the El Quseir, an Egyptian horse-transport ship that was 180 feet shorter and lacked any antennas. Naval historians have labeled this comparison as "physically impossible."

Crucially, the attack only ceased when the Liberty managed to get a distress signal through to the U.S. 6th Fleet. Within minutes of U.S. carrier jets being scrambled, the Israeli forces broke contact.
