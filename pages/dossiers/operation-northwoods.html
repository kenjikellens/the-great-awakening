Executive Summary

Operation Northwoods was a proposed false-flag operation against the Cuban government that originated within the U.S. Department of Defense (DoD) and the Joint Chiefs of Staff (JCS) in 1962. The memorandum, titled "Justification for U.S. Military Intervention in Cuba", outlined a series of simulated and real acts of terrorism—to be conducted on U.S. soil and against U.S. military interests—which would be blamed on the Castro regime. The primary goal was to manufacture a "defensible grievance" that would provide the moral and political justification for a full-scale military invasion of Cuba.

The Lemnitzer Memorandum: Anatomy of a False Flag

The proposal was signed on March 13, 1962, by General Lyman Lemnitzer, Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff, and presented to Secretary of Defense Robert McNamara. The document is characterized by its cold, administrative tone regarding the orchestration of human suffering for strategic gain. The JCS argued that the "pretext" for war must be perceived as a spontaneous provocation by Cuba, rather than a planned U.S. escalation.

The memorandum emphasizes that the operation must be "as natural as possible" and that the "evidence" of Cuban culpability must be undeniable. The Proposed Pretexts: Detailed Proposals

The declassified Northwoods documents contain a specific list of "pretexts" that the U.S. would use to frame Cuba. These included:

Node A: The "Remember the Maine" Scenario

A proposal to blow up a U.S. ship in Guantanamo Bay and blame Cuba. The JCS explicitly referenced the USS Maine incident of 1898 as a template.

Node B: Terrorism in American Cities

"We could develop a Communist Cuban terror campaign in the Miami area, in other Florida cities and even in Washington." The plan suggested placing real bombs to cause deaths, followed by the arrest of "Cuban agents" (operatives in disguise).

Node C: The Drone/Decoy Airplane Hijacking

A complex technical plan to stage the "shooting down" of a civilian airliner. The plan involved an actual flight being replaced by a remote-controlled drone that would distress signals claiming to be under attack by Cuban MiGs.

Kennedy's Rejection & The 1997 Disclosure

President John F. Kennedy rejected the Operation Northwoods proposal in 1962, and shortly thereafter, General Lemnitzer was denied a second term as Chairman. This rejection created a permanent Strategic Friction between the Kennedy administration and the military establishment.

The document remained TOP SECRET for 35 years. It was only identified and released in 1997 by the Assassination Records Review Board (ARRB).
