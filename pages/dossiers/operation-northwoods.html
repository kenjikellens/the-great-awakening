Operation Northwoods

The declassified 1962 proposal for "false flag" attacks on U.S. soil designed to justify a military invasion of Cuba.

Overview

Operation Northwoods was a proposed false-flag operation against the Cuban government that originated within the U.S. Department of Defense (DoD) and the Joint Chiefs of Staff (JCS) in 1962. The plan called for Central Intelligence Agency (CIA) operatives and military assets to stage acts of terrorism in US cities and blame them on Cuba to justify a war that would depose Fidel Castro.

Documented Proposals

The declassified document, signed by Chairman Lyman Lemnitzer, outlined a series of specific "pretexts" including:

  • Self-Attacks: Sinking a U.S. ship in Guantanamo Bay and blaming Cuba.
  • Terrorism in Cities: Staging a "Communist Cuban terror campaign" in the Miami area or even Washington D.C.
  • Airplane Hijackings: Using remote-controlled aircraft to stage the "shooting down" of a civilian airliner.
  • Space Failure: Blaming a potential launch failure of John Glenn's space flight on Cuban interference.

Rejection & Disclosure

President John F. Kennedy rejected the Operation Northwoods proposal in 1962, leading to the eventual removal of General Lemnitzer as Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff. The plan remained classified for 35 years until it was identified by the Assassination Records Review Board (ARRB) in 1997. Since then, Northwoods has become the definitive historical proof that high-level military leadership has considered killing American citizens to achieve geopolitical goals.
