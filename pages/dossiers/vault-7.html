Vault 7 (CIA Hacking Tools)

Dossier Identifier: WL-VAULT7-2017 | Classification: Intelligence & Covert Operations

Leak Metadata

Source AgencyCentral Intelligence Agency (CIA) - Center for Cyber Intelligence
PublisherWikiLeaks (March 2017)
Size8,761 documents & files

Executive Summary

Vault 7 is a vast collection of documents published by WikiLeaks beginning on March 7, 2017. The leak detailed the comprehensive suite of hacking tools, malware, zero-day exploits, and physical infrastructure hacking capabilities developed by the CIA's Center for Cyber Intelligence (CCI) between 2013 and 2016.

The "Year Zero" release exposed the agency's ability to compromise almost all major civilian operating systems and hardware platforms, including Apple iOS, Google Android, Windows, and even smart TVs. The leak fundamentally altered the public understanding of domestic surveillance capabilities and hardware vulnerability.

Documented Evidence: Capabilities

End-to-End Encryption Bypass

A major revelation from Vault 7 was the method by which the CIA bypassed secure messaging applications like WhatsApp, Signal, and Telegram. Instead of attempting to break the app's encryption, the CIA deployed exploits to compromise the underlying operating system (iOS or Android), capturing audio and text logs directly from the device *before* the encryption protocol was applied.

Weeping Angel (Smart TV Hacking)

The "Weeping Angel" program, developed alongside the UK's MI5, was designed to exploit smart TVs (such as Samsung's F-Series). The malware placed the television into a "Fake-Off" mode, deceiving the user into believing the device was disabled while secretly activating the built-in microphone to record ambient room conversation and transmit the audio over the internet.

Vehicle Control Systems

Documents indicated the CIA was researching vectors to infect the computerized control systems used in modern cars and trucks, pointing toward capabilities for untraceable assassinations via induced vehicular malfunction.

Narrative Context: The Absolute Panopticon

Within Great Awakening research, Vault 7 serves as the definitive proof that true digital privacy is an illusion maintained by corporate PR. The leak confirmed that IoT (Internet of Things) devices are actively utilized as domestic intelligence collection nodes.

Furthermore, the CIA's loss of control over these digital weapons—which WikiLeaks stated had circulated among former government hackers and contractors—highlights the intrinsic danger of state-sponsored zero-day hoarding. The subsequent re-designation of WikiLeaks as a "non-state hostile intelligence service" by the CIA emphasizes the extreme sensitivity of this disclosure to the global intelligence community.
