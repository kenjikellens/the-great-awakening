Analyzing the policy-oriented discussion group, its role in fostering global interdependence, and the transition toward expert-led technocratic administration.

Executive Summary

The Trilateral Commission is a non-governmental, policy-oriented discussion group founded in 1973 by David Rockefeller. Its primary purpose is to foster closer cooperation between North America, Europe, and the Asia-Pacific on challenges facing the global order. While marketed as a benign forum for dialogue, research conducted by the Great Awakening project identifies the Commission as the **Institutional Blueprint for Global Technocracy**.

As of 2026, the Trilateral Commission continues to manage the transition of national governance into a more centralized framework. By bringing together financial leaders with former presidents and intelligence directors, the Commission ensures that the Fourth Industrial Revolution is managed by a transnational elite rather than through democratic debate.

The Technetronic Era: Brzezinski’s Intellectual Framework

The intellectual foundation of the Trilateral Commission is found in the work of its first Director, Zbigniew Brzezinski. Brzezinski argued that the international system was moving into an age where computers and telecommunications would allow for a Systemic Micro-Management of the population.

In this "Technetronic Era," traditional democratic participation would be replaced by **Expert-Led Administration**. Brzezinski posited that the complexity of the global economy required a "Global Social Control" mechanism that was beyond the comprehension of the average citizen.

The 1973 Foundation: Atlantic-Pacific Consolidation

The Trilateral Commission was formally launched at a meeting in 1973. David Rockefeller became the primary benefactor. The objective was to bring Japan into the Western security and financial fold, creating a "Trilateral" alliance to manage the post-Gold Standard world.

Research identifies this as the moment the Public-Private Hybrid model of governance became the global standard. The Commission’s role was to ensure that the "rules-based order" favored the expansion of multinational corporations over the interests of sovereign nation-states.

Case Study: The Trilateral "Takeover" of 1976

The most documented instance of Trilateral influence occurred during the 1976 US Presidential Election. Jimmy Carter was selected by the Trilateral Commission as a member in 1973. Following his election, Carter appointed over **20 Trilateral members** to his administration.

  • Zbigniew Brzezinski: National Security Advisor.
  • Cyrus Vance: Secretary of State.
  • Paul Volcker: Chairman of the Federal Reserve.
  • Walter Mondale: Vice President.

This administration oversaw the deregulation of the banking sector and the formalization of the Petrodollar system.

Current Status: 2026 & The Indo-Pacific Pivot

In 2026, the Trilateral Commission has pivoted toward the Indo-Pacific. With the inclusion of members from India and China, the Commission is focused on the Interoperability of Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI).

Discussions in 2025-2026 focus on "Global Resilience" and the creation of **Trans-National Crisis Management Panels**. These panels are designed to bypass national legislatures during "emergencies," effectively handing power to "Expert Commissions."
