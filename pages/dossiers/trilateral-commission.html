Trilateral Commission

The strategic forum for fostering cooperation between the three major global regions.

Overview

The **Trilateral Commission** is a non-governmental, policy-oriented discussion group founded in 1973 by David Rockefeller. It aims to foster closer cooperation between North America, Europe, and Asia (specifically Japan and now China/India) on global challenges and economic integration.

Documented Facts

  • Founding: David Rockefeller, then Chairman of Chase Manhattan Bank, instigated the commission at a meeting in 1972 after a proposal from Zbigniew Brzezinski.
  • High-Level Membership: Its members include former presidents, heads of state, CEOs, high-ranking military officers, and heads of intelligence agencies.
  • The "Technocracy" Narrative: In its early years, critics and biographers—including Barry Goldwater and Antony Sutton—noted the heavy focus on technocratic management of global affairs.

Narrative Analysis

Independent research analyzes the Trilateral Commission as a Technocratic Shadow Cabinet. Its role is perceived as coordinating the transition of national governance into a more centralized, global management structure based on economic and scientific control rather than democratic debate.

"The Trilateral Commission is intended to be the vehicle for multinational consolidation of the commercial and banking interests by seizing control of the political government of the United States." - Barry Goldwater, With No Apologies (1979)

Research Note: Global Interdependence

The commission’s official focus on "global interdependence" is often explored as a narrative for the gradual erosion of national sovereignty in favor of specialized boards and transnational commissions that manage world resources, energy, and security.
