Project MK-Ultra

The CIA's clandestine and illegal program of human experimentation in mind control and behavioral modification.

Overview

MK-Ultra was a top-secret CIA project in which the agency conducted hundreds of clandestine experiments—sometimes on unwitting U.S. and Canadian citizens—to assess the potential use of LSD and other drugs, as well as various methods of torture, for mind control, information gathering, and psychological warfare. It was officially authorized in 1953 and reportedly halted in 1973.

Experimental Methods

Experiments were performed at over 80 institutions, including colleges, universities, hospitals, prisons, and pharmaceutical companies. Methods used to manipulate mental states and brain functions included:

  • Chemicals/Drugs: Covert administration of psychoactive drugs (LSD) to unwitting subjects.
  • Sensory Overload: Use of isolation, deprivation, and electroshock therapy to "break" the subject's personality.
  • Hypnosis & NLP: Early research into neuro-linguistic programming and hypnotic induction.
  • Ablation: Surgical and chemical methods to delete or alter memories.

Destruction of Records

In 1973, with the government-wide panic caused by Watergate, CIA Director Richard Helms ordered all MK-Ultra files destroyed. This made a full historical accounting impossible. However, a small cache of 20,000 documents survived because they had been incorrectly filed as financial records. These became the basis for the 1975 Church Committee and the 1977 Joint Hearing on MK-Ultra where the illegal nature of the project was finally confirmed to the public.
