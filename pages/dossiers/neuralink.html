Neuralink & BCIs

Developing brain-computer interfaces for human-machine integration.

Overview

**Neuralink Corporation** is a neurotechnology company developing implantable brain-computer interfaces (BCIs). The goal is to create a ultra-high bandwidth link between the human brain and external computers, initially to assist with medical conditions like paralysis, but ultimately to enable human "symbiosis" with Artificial Intelligence.

Documented Facts

  • The "Link": The first product is a fully implantable, cosmetically invisible BCI that records and processes neural signals wirelessly.
  • Human Trials: Neuralink received FDA approval for human clinical trials in 2023, with the first human patient implanted in early 2024.
  • Robotic Surgery: Implantation is performed by a specialized surgical robot intended to handle the precise placement of thousands of tiny electrode threads.

Narrative Analysis

Research within the archive explores the Transhumanist Agenda behind Neuralink. While medical benefits are the public-facing motive, the long-term goal of "merging with AI" is analyzed as the ultimate removal of individual biological sovereignty. Critics fear a future where human thoughts can be monitored, uploaded, or manipulated by centralized networks.
