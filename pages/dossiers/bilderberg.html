Bilderberg Group

Documentation on the annual private conference of the North American and European elite, focused on global geopolitical and economic strategy.

Documented Evidence

The Bilderberg Meeting is an annual three-day conference designed to foster dialogue between Europe and North America. Approximately two-thirds of the participants come from Europe and the rest from North America; one-third from government and politics and the rest from other fields.

  • Confidentiality: The meetings are held under the Chatham House Rule, which states that participants are free to use the information received, but neither the identity nor the affiliation of the speaker(s), nor that of any other participant, may be revealed. No minutes are taken and no reports are published.
  • Steering Committee: The group is managed by a permanent Steering Committee, which selects the participants and sets the agenda each year.
  • 2024 Topics: The 70th meeting in Madrid (May 2024) included discussions on AI, Geopolitical Landscape, Climate Challenges, and the Future of Warfare.

Narrative Context

Bilderberg is often analyzed as a "shadow government" forum where global policy is harmonized behind closed doors. Because the participants include heads of state, CEOs of major corporations, and heads of intelligence agencies, critics argue that the private nature of the meetings violates principles of democratic accountability.

The "Great Awakening" research focus is on how these meetings coordinate the implementation of globalist agendas (such as the Great Reset or CBDCs) by aligning the interests of the most powerful private and public stakeholders before policies are presented to national parliaments.
