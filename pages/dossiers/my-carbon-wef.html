WEF: 'My Carbon' & COVID Test

Analyzing the proposal for individual carbon tracking and the institutional perspective on pandemic-era social compliance.

The 'My Carbon' Proposal

In September 2022, the World Economic Forum (WEF) published an article titled "‘My Carbon’: An approach for inclusive and sustainable cities." Written by Kunal Kumar, the piece advocates for a technological shift to track and limit personal carbon footprints within urban environments.

The article identifies three key developments that could enable this transition: 1) COVID-19 as a proof of social responsibility, 2) The Fourth Industrial Revolution (IoT/AI) enabling personal tracking, and 3) Increased awareness of climate change.

"COVID-19 was the test"

The dossier highlights a specific quote that has gained significant public attention regarding the use of crisis management as a pilot for future climate policies:

"COVID-19 was the test of social responsibility – A huge number of unimaginable restrictions for public health were adopted by billions of citizens across the world. There were numerous examples globally of maintaining social distancing, wearing masks, mass vaccinations and acceptance of contact-tracing applications for public health, which demonstrated the core of individual social responsibility."

Critics argue that this framing suggests public health emergencies are viewed by certain institutions as "stress tests" for the implementation of broader behavioral control mechanisms.

Technological Implementation

The proposal relies on the integration of smart city technologies to monitor individual choices in real-time. This includes:

  • Personal Carbon Apps: Applications that calculate emissions based on purchasing and movement data.
  • Digital Compliance: Leveraging the infrastructure built during the pandemic (health passes/tracking) for environmental goals.
  • Social Responsibility Scores: Transitioning from legal mandates to a social-pressure model based on personal data transparency.

It is important to distinguish between opinion pieces published on the WEF platform and official binding policy. While the 'My Carbon' piece reflects the strategic direction of certain influential stakeholders, it does not currently represent a globally mandated law. However, the existing infrastructure for CBDCs and Digital IDs provides the technical foundation for such a system.
