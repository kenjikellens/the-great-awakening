"You Will Own Nothing"

The 'Sharing Economy' and the predicted transition to a rental-only society.

Overview

The phrase **"You'll own nothing and you'll be happy"** surfaced as one of eight predictions by the World Economic Forum for the year 2030. While presented by the WEF as a vision of a efficient, resource-sharing "circular economy," it has become a central point of concern for researchers focused on individual sovereignty and property rights.

Documented Facts

  • Origin: The prediction appeared in a 2016 video titled "8 Predictions for the World in 2030" and an accompanying essay by Ida Auken regarding city life in 2030.
  • Product-as-a-Service: The concept argues that everything will become a service—cars, homes, appliances, and even clothes—rented from platforms rather than owned by individuals.
  • The Sharing Economy: This model leverages digital platforms and the "Internet of Things" (IoT) to track and manage the use of assets in real-time.

Narrative Analysis

Researchers analyze this shift as the **Feudalism of the 21st Century**. By removing the ability to own property, individuals become entirely dependent on a layer of corporate and technocratic "platform owners" for their basic survival and movement.

"Welcome to the year 2030. Welcome to my city - or should I say, 'our city'. I don't own anything. I don't own a car. I don't own a house. I don't own any appliances or any clothes." - Opening of Ida Auken's original WEF essay.

Research Note: Financial Enslavement

Wait, if you "own nothing," your access to life's necessities is contingent on a subscription—one that can be toggled based on your **Social Credit** or **ESG score**. This creates a state of permanent financial vulnerability and dependence on the centralized digital IDs required to access these "services."
