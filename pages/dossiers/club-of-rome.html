Executive Summary

The Club of Rome is an international think tank composed of current and former heads of state, UN bureaucrats, high-level politicians, and scientists. Founded in 1968, it gained global prominence with its first report, The Limits to Growth (1972), which used computer modeling to argue that infinite economic growth is impossible on a finite planet. Research within the Great Awakening project identifies the Club of Rome as the **Intellectual Architect of Neo-Malthusianism**.

As of 2026, the Club of Rome continues to shape the global narrative of "Planetary Emergency." Through initiatives like Earth4All and the Planetary Emergency Plan 2.0, the organization advocates for a radical "Giant Leap" in the global economic system—centered on resource centralization, circular bio-economies, and the transition away from individual ownership (aligned with the WEF’s "You will own nothing" vision).

The 1968 Foundation: The Global Problematique

The Club was founded at the Accademia dei Lincei in Rome by Aurelio Peccei (an Italian industrialist with Fiat) and Alexander King (a British scientist). Their core concept was the **"Global Problematique"**—the idea that the world's problems (pollution, population, resource depletion) are interconnected and cannot be solved by individual nations acting in their own interest.

From its inception, the Club of Rome positioned itself as the "expert" body that would provide the scientific justification for global governance. It pioneered the use of **System Dynamics** modeling at MIT to predict the collapse of civilization unless human consumption was centrally managed. This modeling serves as the technical precursor to the "Net Zero" and "Sustainable Development" targets of the 21st century.

Case Study: The "Common Enemy" Narrative (1991)

In their 1991 report, "The First Global Revolution", the Club of Rome provided a definitively declassified insight into their strategy for achieving global cooperation. Facing the end of the Cold War and the disappearance of the "Red Menace" as a unifying force, the authors explicitly discussed the Creation of a Common Enemy.

"In searching for a common enemy against whom we can unite, we came up with the idea that pollution, the threat of global warming, water shortages, famine and the like, would fit the bill... in designating these dangers as the enemy, we fall into the trap... the real enemy then is humanity itself." (Page 75)

This document proves that the "Environmental Crisis" was selected as the Psychological Catalyst for global integration. By framing humanity as a "plague" upon the planet, the Club of Rome justifies the implementation of technocratic control systems (Carbon Credits, 15-Minute Cities, Bio-Digital Survellance) as necessary "defense mechanisms" for the planetary ecosystem.

Earth4All: The Strategy for "The Giant Leap" (2022-2026)

In 2022, to mark the 50th anniversary of Limits to Growth, the Club released Earth4All: A Survival Guide for Humanity. This report outlines two scenarios: "Too Little Too Late" and "The Giant Leap." The "Giant Leap" is the Club's endorsement of the Great Reset, requiring:

  • Redistribution of Wealth: Implementation of a global progressive taxation system linked to "planetary boundary" compliance.
  • The Energy Transformation: Immediate phasing out of fossil fuels in favor of a centralized "Green Grid" managed by smart contracts (see CBDC dossier).
  • Economic Reframing: Moving away from GDP as a measure of success in favor of "Well-being" metrics, which allow for the rationing of resources based on social credit scores.

Current Status: 2026 & The End of Population Growth

In early 2026, the Club of Rome published a landmark report titled "The End of Population Growth." This research shift acknowledges that global birth rates are falling faster than previously modeled. However, rather than viewing this as a solution to "overpopulation," the Club uses this data to argue for Radical Demographic Governance.

The 2026 strategy focuses on managing the "aging population" through the Biological Redesign of society. This includes the integration of AI-driven elder care and the promotion of "trans-humanist" longevity treatments reserved for the productive "contributing members" of the digital economy. This aligns with the "Resource Rationing" models first proposed in 1972.
