Digital ID (eIDAS)

The centralized framework for verifying individual sovereignty through digital biometric wallets.

Overview

eIDAS (Electronic Identification, Authentication, and Trust Services) is the foundational European Union regulation that enables individuals and businesses to use their own national electronic identification schemes (eIDs) to access public services in other EU countries. In 2024, the framework was evolved into **eIDAS 2.0**, introducing the mandatory "European Digital Identity Wallet."

The Digital Identity Wallet

The new Wallet is a mobile application that allows citizens to store more than just their ID. It integrates various "attributes" into a single, biometrically secured platform, including:

  • Educational Credentials: University degrees and diplomas.
  • Health Records: Vaccination status and digital health certificates.
  • Financial Assets: Integration with CBDCs and bank accounts.
  • Governance Access: Direct authentication for voting or signing public petitions.

Technocratic Analysis

Critical researchers identify eIDAS as the "Gateway to the Social Credit System." By consolidating all aspects of human activity — health, education, travel, and finance — into a single government-controlled application, the state gains the technical ability to grant or revoke access to societal participation in real-time. This "de-platforming" risk is seen as the ultimate deterrent against political dissent.

The transition toward "Passwordless" authentication is also seen as a move to make biometric scanning (face/retina/fingerprint) the only viable way to participate in the digital economy.
