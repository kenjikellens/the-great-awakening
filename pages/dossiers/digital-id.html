Digital ID & Wallet

Analyzing the implementation of interoperable digital identity frameworks and their role in the centralization of personal credentials and financial access.

Documented Evidence

The transition toward mandatory or semi-mandatory Digital ID systems is being coordinated at the highest levels of governance, with the European Union leading the legislative implementation.

  • EU Regulation 2024/1183: This regulation mandates that all EU Member States must provide a Digital Identity Wallet to their citizens by 2026. The wallet will contain identity data, driving licenses, diplomas, and bank account information, allowing for both public and private sector verification.
  • Interoperability: The eIDAS 2.0 framework ensures that a digital identity issued in one state is recognized across the entire Union, creating a unified digital administrative space.
  • WEF "Reimagining Digital ID": The World Economic Forum outlines Digital ID as a prerequisite for participating in the digital economy, enabling "seamless" access to travel, healthcare, and financial services through biometric and cryptographic verification.

Narrative Context

In Great Awakening research, Digital ID is categorized as the "Digital Leash." While promoted as a tool for convenience and inclusion, researchers argue that it serves as the essential infrastructure for a social credit system. By consolidating all personal credentials into a single digital wallet that can be remotely disabled or restricted, governments and corporations gain unprecedented leverage over individual behavior.

The concern is the "all-or-nothing" nature of the system: if access to basic services (banking, travel, communication) is contingent on a valid Digital ID, then the loss of that ID—due to political dissent, a low social score, or technical error—effectively results in a "digital death" where the individual is locked out of modern society.
