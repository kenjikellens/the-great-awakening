WHO Pandemic Agreement (CA+)

Dossier Identifier: WHO-CA-2025 | Classification: Global Health Governance

Framework Metadata

Primary EntityWorld Health Organization (WHO)
Related FrameworksIHR 2005 (Amended 2024), One Health
Adoption StatusAdopted by 78th WHA (May 2025)
Implementation MechanismPathogen Access and Benefit-Sharing (PABS)

Executive Summary

The WHO Pandemic Agreement (CA+) is a global treaty adopted by the World Health Assembly in May 2025. Running parallel to the recently amended International Health Regulations (IHR), the treaty attempts to establish a legally binding architecture for comprehensive pandemic prevention, preparedness, and response under the direct coordination of the World Health Organization.

While officially presented as a mechanism to ensure equitable access to health products, the agreement establishes significant precedents regarding the centralization of health mandates, the tracking of pathogens via the PABS system, and the aggressive monitoring of biological ecosystems through the "One Health" approach.

Documented Evidence: Regulatory Scope

The treaty operates in conjunction with the International Health Regulations (IHR) Amendments (active since Sept 2025), which introduced the "Pandemic Emergency" declaration capability. The CA+ expands on this by creating a permanent bureaucracy intended to oversee the entire pandemic cycle.

Key Treaty Components

  • Pathogen Access and Benefit-Sharing (PABS): An ongoing negotiation intended to force member states to submit domestic pathogen samples to global UN-affiliated labs in exchange for vaccine distribution quotas.
  • One Health Integration: A framework combining human health, animal husbandry, and climate data. This legally broadens the WHO’s jurisdiction, allowing climate change and agriculture to be classified as public health emergencies.
  • Misinformation Protocols: Mandates that signatory nations establish domestic programs to counter "misinformation, disinformation, and false news," primarily regarding medical countermeasures.

Narrative Context: Medical Sovereignty

Researchers highlight the WHO Pandemic Agreement as the most overt attempt to establish a global technocracy through the vector of public health. By combining the "One Health" climate-health approach with the new "Pandemic Emergency" powers of the IHR, critics argue the WHO has effectively granted itself the authority to mandate lockdowns, enforce digital health passports (linked to DPI infrastructures), and dictate domestic medical policies over sovereign national governments.

The mandate to police "misinformation" is viewed as a direct architecture for internet censorship, ensuring that only WHO-approved narratives circulate during a crisis.
