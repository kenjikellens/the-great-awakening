ID2020 Alliance

The global public-private partnership aimed at establishing a universal, persistent digital identity for every human on Earth.

Overview

The ID2020 Alliance is a coalition of public and private sector partners dedicated to ensuring that everyone on the planet has a legal, digital identity. Founding partners include Microsoft, the Rockefeller Foundation, Accenture, Gavi (the Vaccine Alliance), and Ideo.org. While ostensibly a humanitarian effort to help the 1.1 billion people without ID, researchers analyze it as the technical foundation for a global surveillance grid.

Immunization as the Entry Point

One of the most notable strategies of ID2020 is using vaccination programs as the primary "enrolment" mechanism for digital identity. By linking a child's immunization record to a biometric profile from birth, the Alliance aims to create a "portable, persistent, and privacy-protecting" digital ID that follows the individual throughout their entire life. Key projects include:

  • The Bangladesh Pilot: Integrating biometric ID with Gavi-led childhood vaccinations.
  • Global Health Passports: The conceptual predecessor to the digital certificates used during the COVID-19 pandemic.

Technocratic Analysis

Critics argue that ID2020 represents a "Global Biometric Enclosure." By making digital identity a prerequisites for medical care (vaccinations), the Alliance effectively forces the world’s most vulnerable populations into a centralized digital registry. This research highlights the risk of "Identity as a Service" (IDaaS), where the ability to participate in the economy is leased from private technology providers like Microsoft.

The transition toward "Self-Sovereign Identity" (SSI) is often analyzed as a rhetorical device used to disguise the fact that the underlying blockchains are managed by centralized technocratic actors.
