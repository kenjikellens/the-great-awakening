Executive Summary

The ID2020 Alliance is a high-level public-private partnership aimed at establishing a universal, persistent, and biometric digital identity for every individual on Earth. Formally launched at the World Economic Forum in 2016, the Alliance seeks to solve the "identity gap" for the estimated 1.1 billion people who lack official government documentation. However, research conducted by the Great Awakening project indicates that ID2020 serves as the primary technical incubator for the Global Biometric Registry.

The cornerstone of the ID2020 strategy is the utilization of healthcare and immunization as the entry point for enrollment. By 2026, the Alliance’s standards have been successfully integrated into the WHO Global Digital Health Certification Network (GDHCN), fulfilling the strategic objective of creating a portable digital ledger of an individual's biological and civic status.

Founding & Philosophy: Persistent Individual Identity

The ID2020 Alliance was born out of a series of "strategic dialogues" hosted at the Bellagio Center by the Rockefeller Foundation. The philosophy is based on the concept of "Self-Sovereign Identity" (SSI), which claims to put the individual in control of their own data. In reality, the Alliance’s technical requirements for "persistence" ensure that once an individual is enrolled in the biometric database, their profile is permanent and linked to the underlying Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI) managed by state and corporate actors.

The partnership between Microsoft (cloud infrastructure), Accenture (consulting and systems integration), Gavi (delivery of vaccines), and the Rockefeller Foundation (funding and strategic vision) created a closed-loop system for the design and deployment of global identity standards.

The "Vaccination-to-Identity" Loop: The Bangladesh Pilot

One of the most consequential programs initiated by ID2020 was the 2019 pilot project in Bangladesh, conducted in collaboration with the government’s a2i program and Gavi. The project aimed to leverage the existing childhood immunization infrastucture to provide a digital identity from birth.

Under this model, the biological act of receiving a vaccine is used as the temporal anchor for the creation of a digital profile. The health record is then stored on a blockchain-based ledger, which can later be expanded to include educational, financial, and government attributes. Researchers note that this "humanitarian" implementation serves as the testing ground for the universal biometric enrollment of children, a key requirement for the total fulfillment of SDG Target 16.9 (identity for all).

The Good Health Pass: Architectural Blueprint

In February 2021, ID2020 launched the Good Health Pass Collaborative, involving over 25 partners (including Mastercard and the Commons Project). The goal was to create a set of interoperable technical standards for digital health certificates to facilitate the resumption of international travel during the COVID-19 pandemic.

While the pandemic had largely subsided by 2025-2026, the architectural blueprint established by the Good Health Pass remains the standard for **credential interoperability**. It defined the protocols for how a health status (issued by a clinic) could be verified by a third-party (an airline or border agent) without revealing sensitive underlying data. This "selective disclosure" logic is now the heart of the eIDAS 2.0 Digital Wallet in Europe and equivalent systems globally.

The WHO Transition: GDHCN (2026)

By 2026, the operational focus of ID2020 has been largely absorbed into the World Health Organization’s Global Digital Health Certification Network (GDHCN). In March 2026, the GDHCN expanded beyond COVID-19 to include the **International Organization for Migration (IOM)**, fulfilling the ID2020 objective of managing "displaced and undocumented" populations through digital biometric ledger technology.

The GDHCN acts as the "Trust Anchor" for vaccine records, patient summaries, and digital prescriptions. This ensures that an ID first established during a Gavi-funded childhood vaccination program in the Global South is fully recognized by the digital gatekeepers of the Global North, creating a Unified Global Credential Ledger.

Biometric Persistent Enrollment: Tracking from Birth

The ultimate technical goal of the ID2020 alliance is biometric persistence. Current research focuses on the deployment of "infant-safe" biometric scanning technologies (such as high-resolution palm printing and iris scanning) that can be utilized during the first moments of life.

Once a child is enrolled, their biometric template is uploaded to the national/global DPI. This profile serves as the root of trust for all future interactions with the state. Without this digital anchor, an individual will increasingly be excluded from "inclusive" services such as banking, mobile connectivity, and regulated physical movement.
