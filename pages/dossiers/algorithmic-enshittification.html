Algorithmic Enshittification

The systematic degradation of digital platforms to extract maximum value and control.

Overview

"Enshittification" is a term coined by writer Cory Doctorow to describe the lifecycle of successful online platforms. The process occurs in three stages: first, platforms are good to their users; then they abuse their users to make things better for their business customers; finally, they abuse those business customers to claw back all the value for themselves. Then, they die.

In the context of the "Great Awakening" research, we analyze this not just as a business model, but as a deliberate strategy of information management.

Documented Facts

Platforms like Facebook, Google, and Amazon have all shown patterns of increasing monetization of basic functionalities. Features that were once free and user-centric are now increasingly hidden behind paywalls or algorithmic filters that prioritize high-paying advertisers or politically favored content.

Shadowbanning: The practice of blocking or partially blocking a user or their content from an online community so that it is not readily apparent that the user is being silenced.

Narrative Analysis

The "Great Awakening" perspective views enshittification as the planned obsolescence of the free internet. By making the independent web unusable through spam-filled search results and throttled social reach, big tech "enclosures" can force the population into unified "walled gardens." These gardens are easier to monitor, censor, and integrate into broader social credit and digital identity systems.

"Enshittification is the burning of the commons to build the digital castle. It is the end of the internet as a tool for liberation and its rebirth as a system of total surveillance." - Platform Ethics Analysis

Research Note: AI Integration

We track how the integration of Generative AI into search and social feeds accelerates the enshittification process by replacing primary sources with AI-filtered summaries, further distancing the user from unfiltered information.
