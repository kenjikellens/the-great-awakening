Executive Summary

Cyber Polygon was a series of international cybersecurity training events held in 2020 and 2021. Organized jointly by the World Economic Forum (WEF), Russian cybersecurity firm BI.ZONE, and Interpol, the event simulated comprehensive cyberattacks against critical global infrastructure.

The overarching theme of the exercise was the threat of a "Cyber Pandemic"—a cascaded failure of global supply chains, financial systems, and public health networks resulting from targeted cyber intrusions. While officially framed as a resilience-building exercise, independent research draws parallels between this simulation and the biological pandemic simulation, Event 201.

Documented Evidence: The "Cyber Pandemic"

In his opening remarks at the 2020 Cyber Polygon event, WEF Executive Chairman Klaus Schwab stated explicitly that a comprehensive cyberattack could cause a systemic shock "with characteristics of a COVID-like crisis." The simulation actively trained corporate "blue teams" to defend against such systemic collapses.

Focus Areas (2021 Iteration)

  • Supply Chain Security: Mitigating risks posed by interconnected corporate networks.
  • Digital Currencies: Securing the development of upcoming Central Bank Digital Currencies (CBDCs).
  • Centralized Response: Encouraging a unified, global regulatory approach to managing internet infrastructure during a crisis.

Narrative Context: Anticipatory Architectures

Within Great Awakening research frameworks, Cyber Polygon is frequently categorized alongside Event 201 as an "anticipatory architecture." The theory suggests that the solutions proposed during these "simulations"—such as the consolidation of internet infrastructure, the implementation of robust biometric verification for internet access, and the phasing out of cash to prevent digital theft—are pre-planned global governance models waiting for a catalytic trigger.

The WEF's emphasis on a centralized, multi-stakeholder governance model for the internet raises concerns regarding digital sovereignty, online anonymity, and the potential for a "digital kill switch" justified by the threat of a cyber pandemic.
