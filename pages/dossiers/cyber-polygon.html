Cyber Polygon

Analyzing the international cyber-attack simulation designed to test the resilience of the global digital ecosystem and supply chains.

Documented Evidence

Cyber Polygon is an annual international training exercise for internal teams to practice responding to cyber-attacks. It is organized by BI.ZONE (a subsidiary of Sberbank) in collaboration with the World Economic Forum and INTERPOL.

  • 2021 Simulation: The 2021 event focused on a "cyber pandemic" scenario where a targeted attack on a single supply chain element could result in the total shutdown of the global digital infrastructure. Participants included major international banks, tech companies, and state agencies.
  • Centre for Cybersecurity: The WEF's Centre for Cybersecurity uses Cyber Polygon as a primary platform to promote the concept of "cyber resilience" and the need for standardized global regulations in the digital space.
  • Global Cooperation: The exercise emphasizes the necessity for public-private partnerships and the centralization of cyber-defense strategies across national borders to mitigate systemic risks to the internet.

Narrative Context

In the Great Awakening research community, Cyber Polygon is analyzed as the "predictive programming" or a trial run for a massive, state-sponsored cyber-attack. Similar to how Event 201 preceded the COVID-19 pandemic, researchers are concerned that Cyber Polygon prepares the public for a "Cyber Pandemic" that would justify the total shutdown of the traditional internet in favor of a "secured" and identity-verified digital network.

The core narrative focus is on the potential for a "forced digital transition." By simulating a collapse of the supply chain, globalists could theoretically justify a "reset" of the internet, where all participants must have a Digital ID to access financial and social services, thereby ending digital anonymity and cementing technocratic control over the flow of information.
