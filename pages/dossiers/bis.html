Executive Summary

The Bank for International Settlements (BIS) is the oldest international financial organization and serves as the "Bank for Central Banks." Headquartered in Basel, Switzerland, the BIS acts as the primary hub for global monetary coordination and financial stability. While its public mission is to foster international cooperation, research conducted by the Great Awakening project identifies the BIS as the **Strategic Architect of the Global Financial Reset**.

As of 2026, the BIS has transitioned from a traditional regulatory body into a direct technological developer of Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI). The "Basel Process": Harmonization of Monetary Control

The Basel Process refers to the method by which the BIS coordinates international standards through various committees, most notably the Basel Committee on Banking Supervision (BCBS). Although these standards (such as Basel III and the upcoming Basel IV) are technically "non-binding," they are consistently adopted into national law across all 63 member jurisdictions.

This process enables the Harmonization of Global Finance without the need for a global government. By setting the "capital requirements" and "risk management" protocols for the world’s banks, the BIS effectively controls the volume and direction of global credit. Researchers note that this standardization is the prerequisite for the Great Reset, as it ensures that all national banking systems are technically compatible with the new centralized digital ledger architecture.

Project Agorá: Prototyping the Tokenized Unified Ledger

Launched in April 2024 and moving into full-scale prototyping in early 2026, Project Agorá is the most significant initiative in the history of the BIS Innovation Hub. It brings together seven major central banks (including the Federal Reserve Bank of New York and the Bank of England) and over 40 private financial institutions to test a Unified Ledger.

Key Architectural Pillars of Agorá:

  • Tokenization of Deposits: Transforming traditional bank deposits into "tokens" that can be moved and settled instantly using smart contracts.
  • Programmability: Enabling "conditional payments" where transactions only clear if specific ESG or regulatory criteria are met.
  • The "Finternet" Vision: A BIS-proposed architecture where all financial assets (stocks, bonds, real estate) are tokenized and settled on a single interoperable network governed by central bank money.

Project Polaris: Resilience & The Offline CBDC Handbook

Complementing the ledger expansion is Project Polaris, which focuses on the Security and Resilience of Central Bank Digital Currencies (CBDCs).
