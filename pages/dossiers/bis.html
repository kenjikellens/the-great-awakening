Bank for International Settlements (BIS)

Investigation into the "Bank for Central Banks," its global monetary authority, and its legal immunity from national jurisdictions.

Documented Evidence

The BIS is an international financial institution owned by central banks that "fosters international monetary and financial cooperation." It is the oldest international financial organization in the world.

  • Legal Immunity: Under its Statutes and host country agreements, the BIS, its property, and assets are immune from seizure, attachment, or execution. Its premises and archives are inviolable, operating effectively outside the legal jurisdiction of national governments.
  • CBDC Coordination: The BIS Innovation Hub is the primary coordinator for global Central Bank Digital Currency (CBDC) research. This includes "Project mBridge," which utilizes a multi-CBDC ledger to enable real-time, cross-border payments between various national central banks.
  • The Basel Committee: The BIS hosts the Basel Committee on Banking Supervision (BCBS), which sets the global standards for bank capital requirements (Basel III/IV), influencing the lending capacity of every bank worldwide.

Narrative Context

Within the Great Awakening framework, the BIS is analyzed as the "apex of the global financial pyramid." Researchers point to its absolute legal immunity and its role in coordinating the worldwide transition to CBDCs as evidence of a non-sovereign, technocratic authority that manages the global money supply without democratic oversight.

The concern is that the BIS provides the centralized digital architecture required to enforce global economic policies, where national central banks act as regional administrators for a single, integrated monetary system managed from Basel.
