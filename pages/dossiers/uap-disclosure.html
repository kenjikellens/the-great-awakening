UAP Disclosure

Tracking the shift in official government policy regarding unidentified phenomena.

Overview

Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena (UAP), formerly referred to as UFOs, have moved from the fringe of conspiracy theory into the realm of official government investigations. Since 2017, triggered by the release of three Navy videos (FLIR, GIMBAL, GOFAST), the US government has significantly increased its public engagement with the subject.

The establishment of the Unidentified Aerial Phenomena Task Force (UAPTF) and its successor, AARO, represents a formalized effort to collect and analyze data on objects that exhibit flight characteristics beyond known human technology.

Documented Facts

The June 2021 ODNI report acknowledged 144 reports of UAP from 2004 to 2021, with 143 remaining "unexplained." The report stated that UAP "clearly pose a safety of flight issue and may pose a challenge to U.S. national security."

Legislative Action: Recent National Defense Authorization Acts (NDAA) have included provisions for whistleblower protection for individuals coming forward with information regarding secret UAP programs.
