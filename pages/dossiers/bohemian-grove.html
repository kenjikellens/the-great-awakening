Bohemian Grove

An exclusive 2,700-acre private retreat for the world's political, corporate, and military elite.

Overview

Bohemian Grove is a restricted campground in Sonoma County, California, belonging to the all-male private Bohemian Club. Every July, the club hosts a two-week "encampment" attended by some of the most powerful men in the world, including former US presidents, CEOs of major defense contractors, and media moguls.

"Weaving Spiders Come Not Here"

The club's motto, "Weaving Spiders Come Not Here," implies that business deals and political discussions should not take place within the grove. However, historical evidence contradicts this. Most notably, the "Lakeside Talk" in 1942 involving the Manhattan Project took place at the Grove, illustrating its role as a precursor to world-changing policy shifts.

The Cremation of Care

The encampment begins with the "Cremation of Care" ceremony — a theatrical production performed in front of a 40-foot stone owl shrine. While proponents describe it as a harmless drama meant to "banish the worries of the world," critical researchers identify the occult-themed imagery and ritualistic nature as a mechanism for reinforcing elite tribalism and psychological bonding among participants.

Research Note

Research into Bohemian Grove focuses on the membership lists and the correlation between the "Lakeside Talks" and subsequent federal appointments. We also track the logistical involvement of major corporate sponsors in maintaining the grove's extreme security perimeter.
